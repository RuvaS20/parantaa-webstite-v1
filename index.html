Who we really are

The information and technology gap is a major barrier preventing many young Zimbabweans from breaking out of the poverty cycle. Parantaa Africa Foundation aims to bridge this gap by:

Tailoring different approaches to the issues and cultures of different communities.

Enabling young people to discover their dreams in sports, arts, and education through opportunity access.

Leveraging our networks all over the world, including MasterCard Foundation Scholars, to nurture the bright futures of young Zimbabweans.

RACHEL DEVICES™ Access

Information Access  | World Possible  | Chitungwiza

Parantaa has channeled funding into acquiring RACHEL DEVICES™ from World Possible. These devices can wirelessly transmit free digital instructional content to nearby tablets, computers, or smartphones without the need for an internet connection or data plan. They will allow at least 50 students at a time to access pre-loaded copies of leading educational websites including Khan Academy, Scratch, TED and other customizable content.

For the 26% of Zimbabwe's primary school students and 43% of high school students without access to the internet, RACHEL devices provide fast, free access to quality educational content. With these portable, plug-and-play server we hope to connect underserved youth to a world of educational content with a single click. We have also availed several laptops for this purpose too, partnering with local organizations for the supervision of their use.

Get to know us

Rufaro Nyoni

Founder

Rufaro is an MBA candidate at Arizona State University and a Baobab scholar. She is a former recipient of the Makomborero Zimbabwe and MasterCard Foundation Scholarships. She graduated from United States International University-Africa with honors in Finance and a concentration in Investment Management. Over the years, Rufaro has found purpose in working with young people from low-income backgrounds helping them identify their passions and talents and linking them with opportunities that can change their lives positively. When not crunching numbers or analyzing financial markets, Rufaro loves singing and meditating on the hammock!

Shyline Bajaba

Trustee

Shyline is a Biochemistry and Cell Biology major at Jacobs University Bremen in Germany. She is a current recipient of the MasterCard Foundation Scholarship and a former recipient of the Makomborero Zimbabwe Scholarship. She has prior experience in the actuarial science field. Shyline dedicates most of her time to working with segregated communities including inmates and ex-inmates. She is passionate about rehabilitation and has found her purpose in assisting inmates to rehabilitate and integrate into society. Her belief in access to opportunities has navigated her to work with organisations dedicated to improving the lives of young people from low-income backgrounds. When not researching criminology, Shyline loves the gym, netball and crochet.

Charmaine Natasha Nyakonda

Trustee

Charmaine is a multiskilled neuroscientist with extensive experience in experience stewardship, stakeholder engagement and partner relationship management. She has been heavily involved in youth-based programming, community-engaged learning and asset-based community development, mental health education and activism. Some questions we're
frequently asked

Is Parantaa Africa Foundation a registered NGO?

Yes. Parantaa Africa Foundation is registered as a Trust by the Registrar of Deeds of Zimbabwe under the Deeds Registries Act Chapter 20:05.

Other than partnerships, how else can I get involved?

Thanks for your interest in supporting Parantaa! We welcome partnerships, interns, volunteers, and we also accept donations. If you'd like to get in touch with us about any of these options, please email us at @parantaafricafoundation.org

When is Parantaa coming to my community?

We are currently operating on-the-ground in Chitungwiza, but we do have plans to expand our reach in the near future. Be sure to follow us on social media to see our calendar events!

How can I volunteer with Parantaa?

We strongly believe in giving back to the community, which is why we welcome community volunteers from wherever. Send us an email if you're interested in taking action!
